## PART 1 — THE UNIFIED NARRATIVE

### One sentence
**Lizzy's Lab is one story told three ways: a person who was never incapable, just uninvited — who built her own invitation, sounds like it (ADHD house), and now sells the tools she built to survive the double life.**

### How the three "projects" collapse into one identity

You don't have three projects. You have one thesis with three expressions:

**The thesis (the masking story):** You spent years running two operating systems — the corporate one that got you stability, competence, and lowered anxiety at Oaktree, and the creative one you kept quiet because you'd decided you weren't smart enough for it. "I was never incapable. Just uninvited" is not a caption. It is the brand's entire POV. Everything ladders to it.

**The proof (the DJ persona):** Lizzy McWired is the thesis made audible. ADHD house — "music that moves the way my brain does" — is what un-masking *sounds* like. The origin carousel, the Algorythm residency, Bruno, Mirjam, the b2b-at-Grand-Park seed: these aren't marketing for the brand. They ARE the brand, running as a live, public demonstration that the thesis is true.

**The product (Lizzy's Lab the business):** The Ritual Kit is the thesis made physical. The "Executive Function Tax" you named in the grant application is the same tax you pay masking at work every day. The NFC coins exist because YOU needed to automate the transition between your two lives. Customer = past you. The grant application already said it perfectly: *"every set I play is a live demonstration of the Ritual Kit's sensory architecture."*

**Where the pilot fits (CORRECTED 7/3):** NOSEBLIND — "The Bravest Soldier" exists as a finished v5 draft with a series bible. It is the source text of the entire universe, not a future container: the check engine light is the thesis ("it never gets fixed; but so am I"), the Waymo is the confessional booth, Sherman is safety-that-reads-as-suspicious, and the "uninvited" arc of the carousel is an excerpt of a story already written in full. The Climb pillar = the pilot serialized for social. The pilot's actual gap is packaging, not writing: logline, one-page pitch, and a submission calendar (labs, fellowships, contests) — scheduled as a September workstream.

### The narrative pyramid (locked June 20, unchanged)
- **Layer 1 — Lizzy's Lab:** the universe. Never changes.
- **Layer 2 — Three pillars:**
  - **The Climb** — origin, double life, masking, corporate↔rave, the "uninvited" arc
  - **The Craft** — DJ practice, ADHD house, production, Bruno, Mirjam, collectives
  - **CEO Era** — the business, Ritual Kit, neurodivergent sensory products (seeded now, launched deliberately)

### The Oaktree insight (this is the new piece)
Your corporate leveling-up isn't background — it's the business's operating system. The person who ran the NY onsite with color-coded strategy one-pagers, built Melanie's Chronos app, and manages legal team logistics is the same person who will run inventory, fulfillment, and event production. The brand story says "double life." The business plan says "dual competency." Both are true, and the second one is your unfair advantage — the April investor analysis flagged execution risk as the killer, and the Oaktree version of you is the answer to it.

## PART 4 — SAFE POCKETS: WELLNESS SYSTEM (added 7/3)

### Design principles
- **Architecture over willpower.** Every pocket is anchored to a physical object, an automation, or another person — never to "remembering."
- **Sustainable taper, not purity.** A missed day is data, not failure. Streaks track *rituals completed*, not perfection.
- **Dogfood the brand.** The transition tools below ARE the Ritual Kit in prototype. Every pocket doubles as CEO-Era R&D and content.
- **Shoot as training camp.** July 4–19 = fourteen days where sleep, skin, and energy have a visible deadline.

### The weed taper (sustainable, three phases)
Context: daily smoker for years. Goal sequence set by Lizbeth herself.

| Phase | Window | Rule | Support structure |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Consolidate** | Now → ~mid-Aug | ONE shared joint, end of day only — *after* landing ritual + dinner, never before work, never alone | Sherman holds/rolls it (shared = built-in accountability). Time-lock container (kSafe-style) set to open 9pm — turns the decision into architecture. Both trigger windows get replacement rituals (below) |
| **2 — Weekends only** | ~Mid-Aug → Sept | Fri/Sat/Sun only, still shared, still end of day | Named weeknight replacement ritual (tea + show, bath, joint dog walk). **Expect 1–2 weeks of rougher sleep and vivid dreams when daily stops — that's normal, temporary, and not a sign it's failing** |
| **3 — Occasions** | Oct+ | Pre-defined special occasions only (define the list in advance, in writing) | Quarterly review decides the list |

Non-negotiable across all phases: **never before work, never solo.** Those two rules alone break the habit's automation.

### The two transition windows (former smoke triggers)
- **Morning (before work):** dogs out + 10 min movement/sun. NFC tag by the door (see tools) fires the sequence.
- **Evening (arriving home):** shoes off → tap door tag → 15-min space reset timer → dinner → *then* the day is allowed to end. The reset IS the decompression; the joint (Phase 1) comes after, not instead.

### Weekly pockets
- Gym Tue/Thu, yoga Sun (or swap days — but they're named and calendared)
- 4 home dinners/week from a fixed 3-meal lazy roster (no deciding, just rotating)
- Sunday 30-min review: dashboard + next week's food/gym slots + streak check

### Tool stack (AI + automation + physical)
**AI**
1. **Claude reminders + calendar:** transition-window nudges, gym/yoga blocks, 7/12 hair deadline — set directly in Reminders/Calendar
2. **Gemini patron-saints poster:** the six saints in the Steve Madden caricature style, printed and hung where the rituals happen. Manifestation you can see
3. **Claude as Sunday copilot:** paste the week, get next week's plan + dinner roster refresh
4. **Dashboard "Pockets" tab:** streak counters wired into maycontent.netlify.app, patron-saint lines rotated into Morning Mode messages

**Automation (this is literally your product category)**
5. **Prototype your own Ritual Kit with ~$10 of NFC stickers:** door tag = landing ritual (timer + playlist + log), nightstand tag = sleep mode (alarm set, brightness down, Do Not Disturb), fridge tag = opens the dinner roster. You become customer zero — and every tap is CEO-Era content and product R&D
6. **iPhone Screen Time downtime at 10:30pm** + time-based Shortcut automations at both trigger windows

**Physical**
7. Time-lock container for the jar (opens 9pm, Phase 1)
8. Sunrise alarm lamp — consistent wake without the phone
9. Wall chain calendar by the door (don't break the chain — mark rituals, not perfection)
10. Gym bag staged by the door the night before; yoga mat visible, never stored
